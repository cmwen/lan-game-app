# 👤 User Personas — Party Pocket

## Persona 1: "The Organizer" — Alex, 27

**Role in App**: Primary Host

> "I just want everyone to have a good time and not spend 20 minutes figuring out how to connect."

### Background
- Hosts monthly game nights for 4–8 friends
- Has a Nintendo Switch, owns Mario Party and Jackbox
- Android phone (Pixel 7), comfortable with tech
- Gets frustrated when guests have to "download an app" — so they love that everyone already has Android

### Goals
- Get everyone into a game in under 2 minutes
- Look cool for "discovering" this app and introducing it to friends
- Have a go-to game that impresses people who haven't seen it before

### Pain Points
- Jackbox requires everyone to open a browser + type a room code + wait for game host to navigate menus
- Nintendo Switch means someone always has to sit out
- Some apps require accounts or sign-ins which kills the vibe

### How Alex Uses Party Pocket
1. Opens app at the start of the party
2. Taps "Host a Game" — shows QR code to the group
3. Waits 30 seconds for everyone to join (loves watching names pop in)
4. Hits "Start" and the chaos begins

### Alex's Delight Moment
When the first Tilt Racer race ends and someone falls off their chair laughing at how they physically tilted their whole body trying to win.

## Persona 2: "The Skeptic" — Jordan, 24

**Role in App**: Reluctant Player → Converted Fan

> "Is this actually fun or are we just going to stare at our phones?"

### Background
- Generally skeptical of mobile games ("they're all garbage")
- Would rather play a board game or Switch
- Gets handed Alex's QR code and reluctantly scans it
- Within 90 seconds, is completely engaged

### Goals
- Not be bored at a party
- Beat their friends (secretly competitive)
- Avoid anything that feels embarrassing or confusing

### Pain Points
- Mobile games feel cheap and watered-down
- Hates reading instructions — wants instant clarity
- Gives up on games with long tutorials

### How Jordan Uses Party Pocket
1. Scans QR code with zero enthusiasm
2. Types name: "JORDAN 💀"
3. Sees "Tap as fast as possible!" and immediately understands — no explanation needed
4. Loses Tap Frenzy by 3 taps and wants a rematch

### Jordan's Delight Moment
Getting a false start in Reaction Roulette and seeing -1 points appear, then screaming at the injustice. Immediately wants revenge.

## Persona 3: "The Newcomer" — Sam, 19

**Role in App**: First-time Mobile Party Gamer

> "I don't really play video games but this looks fun I guess?"

### Background
- Not a gamer; uses their phone for social media and music
- Has never played WarioWare, Mario Party, or Jackbox
- Joins because their friend group is playing and feels left out otherwise
- Low tolerance for complexity or feeling "bad at games"

### Goals
- Not feel stupid or embarrassed
- Actually participate and feel included
- Have fun even if they're not competitive

### Pain Points
- Complex game rules make them feel excluded
- Small text and tiny touch targets on mobile
- Games that require dexterity they don't have

### How Sam Uses Party Pocket
1. Borrows a phone and types in the room code
2. Chooses the name "Samantha 🌸"
3. Reads "Hold your phone still!" and immediately gets it — even beats the competitive players
4. Ends up winning Hold Steady (v1.1) on their first try because they're naturally calm

### Sam's Delight Moment
Accidentally winning a game through instinct rather than skill, and seeing their name at the top of the leaderboard.

## Persona 4: "The Fanatic" — Chris, 22

**Role in App**: Power User / Evangelist

> "I've played every party game ever made. Let me see what you've got."

### Background
- WarioWare expert, Jackbox Quiplash master, 1-2-Switch champion
- Has very high expectations; will immediately compare to professional games
- The first person in the group to critique anything broken
- Also the first person to share something genuinely good with their entire friend group

### Goals
- Find a game that challenges the group with clever mechanics
- Discover hidden depth in simple-looking games
- Share something genuinely impressive

### Pain Points
- Party games that have only 2-3 games get boring fast
- Poor network reliability (latency, disconnects mid-game) kills the mood
- Low production value (no sound, no animations) feels amateur

### How Chris Uses Party Pocket
1. Takes over as host from Alex "just to test it properly"
2. Immediately tests the edge cases (disconnecting and reconnecting)
3. Finds the skill ceiling in Tilt Racer (optimizing tilt angle)
4. Demands more games — becomes the primary requestor for v1.1 features

### Chris's Delight Moment
Discovering that Reaction Roulette has depth — that timing the false start risk is actually a strategic decision, not pure luck.
